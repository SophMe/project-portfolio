Mata Hari

A woman who danced with mystery and defied convention

Mata Hari in 1906

1876 – Birth

Mata Hari was born as Margaretha Geertruida Zelle on August 7, 1876, in Leeuwarden, Netherlands.

1895 – Marriage

At the age of 18, she married Dutch colonial army officer Rudolf MacLeod and moved to Java and Sumatra in the Dutch East Indies.

1903 – Divorce

After a turbulent marriage and the tragic death of their son, Mata Hari returned to Europe and divorced Rudolf MacLeod.

1905 – Rise as an Exotic Dancer

She reinvented herself as Mata Hari, an exotic dancer in Paris, drawing widespread attention for her performances inspired by the East Indies.

1914 – World War I

When World War I broke out, Mata Hari’s life took a dramatic turn as she became involved in espionage, allegedly working as a double agent for Germany and France.

1917 – Arrest

Mata Hari was arrested by French authorities and accused of being a spy for Germany, leading to one of the most famous spy trials in history.

1917 – Execution

On October 15, 1917, Mata Hari was executed by firing squad in France. Her life and death remain shrouded in mystery and intrigue.
